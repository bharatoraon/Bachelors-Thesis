SCENARIO C — COMBINED INTEGRATION
~30

Timetable Synchronisation + Physical FOB Infrastructure (Combined)

The combined scenario achieves the greatest overall TFI reduction. Critically, the analysis reveals that operational interventions (Schedule Sync) yield far higher structural friction reduction than physical walkway investments alone. Infrastructure alone cannot overcome peripheral peripheral isolation caused by 45–90-minute headways on suburban rail. Timetable integration is the primary lever — FOBs amplify but cannot substitute.

🏆 Policy Priority: Operational schedule integration FIRST → then targeted infrastructure at high-friction nodes
💡
Key Conclusions

4 Core Evidenced Conclusions

C1
Transfer friction in Chennai is not evenly distributed — it is a spatially inequitable burden concentrated in suburban rail nodes at the periphery.
C2
Peak-hour friction penalties (up to 90+ min) at VOC Nagar & Veppampattu represent a temporal inequity trap for outer-city commuters.
C3
Timetable Synchronisation is the most impactful single policy lever — alone reducing average TFI by ~40% without any physical construction.
C4
Gate-to-gate polygon analysis proves centroid methods underestimate walk friction at metro/rail nodes, invalidating prior spatial analyses.
📋
Policy Recommendations
P1

Unified Timetable Integration Authority

Establish a statutory inter-agency body (CMRL–TNSTC–Southern Railway) to mandate coordinated departure schedules. Max 15-min cross-modal wait at all interchange nodes.

P2

Peripheral Suburban Rail Frequency Fix

Increase off-peak Southern Railway headways from 45–90 min to ≤20 min on high-TFI corridors — targeting VOC Nagar, Veppampattu, Pattaravakkam.

P3

Targeted FOB Construction at Hotspots

Prioritise physical walkway improvements (FOBs, subways) at nodes where walk distance exceeds 250m — specifically high-TFI bus-rail transfer points in southern and northern corridors.

P4

Real-Time TFI Monitoring Dashboard

Adopt the TFI framework as a statutory transit equity metric in Chennai's MaaS platform — enabling continuous temporal inequity monitoring and data-driven schedule revision.
