01The Transfer Friction Index — Formula Display
Transfer Friction Index (TFI) — Novel Composite Metric for Multimodal Interchange Nodes
TFI  =  Wt  +  Ww  +  (Pmiss  ×  Ww) Output in minutes per transfer node
Wt
Gate-to-Gate Walk Time
Physical walk duration from exit gate of Mode A to entry gate of Mode B. Computed as: distance (metres) ÷ 1.2 m/s (HCM standard pedestrian speed). Measured from precise GeoJSON polygon gate centroids — not station centroids.
Ww
Expected Wait Time — Headway Proxy
Average inter-departure headway at the receiving stop for the connecting mode. Computed from GTFS stop_times.txt as chronological differences between successive departures. Filtered: 0 < headway < 3 hours. Default 15 min where data is sparse.
Pmiss × Ww
Missed Connection Penalty — Expected Value
Expected additional wait if the connecting vehicle is missed. Probability scales jointly with walk duration and service headway. Multiplied by Ww because missing a vehicle incurs one additional full headway of waiting time.
02Intellectual Thought Process — How the Formula Was Built
1
What time costs does a passenger actually pay at a transfer?
Starting from first principles: identify all time costs unique to a transfer event — distinct from in-vehicle time. Three emerged: walk, wait, and missed connection risk.
2
What does existing literature say about transfer cost decomposition?
Wardman (2004), Guo & Wilson (2011) — the Generalised Cost framework decomposes travel cost as walk + wait + penalty terms. All three terms confirmed in international literature.
3
What data is available for Chennai — what can be computed?
GTFS provides walk distance and headways. Gate GeoJSON provides accurate walk. No real-time data available — must use a probabilistic reliability proxy for the third term.
4
How to operationalise reliability risk as a computable scalar?
Bates et al. (2001), Liu & Sinha (2007): miss probability scales with walk duration AND headway. Formulated as a product, clipped to realistic range [0.05 – 0.40].
5
Is the formula complete, non-redundant, and comparable across all 8,000+ edges?
Each term is orthogonal and captures a distinct passenger cost. Output is in minutes — a universal unit enabling comparison across modes, zones, and temporal windows.

06The Completeness Argument

A transfer involves exactly three and only three time costs a passenger pays — in strict chronological sequence. TFI captures all three without double-counting any component.

Stage Passenger Action Formula Term Nature of Cost Measurement Basis
Stage 1 Exits Mode A; walks to Mode B boarding point Wt Deterministic Gate-to-gate distance ÷ 1.2 m/s
Stage 2 Reaches platform; stands and waits for Mode B Ww Probabilistic avg. GTFS headway at receiving stop
Stage 3 If walk ran long, misses vehicle; waits additional time Pmiss × Ww Expected value Joint probability × headway penalty
Each term is mutually exclusive: Wt occurs prior to arrival; Ww occurs at the platform; Pmiss×Ww is an expected value — not a second wait added on top. The formula is complete and non-redundant.
07Viva Examination Defence — Four Anticipated Questions
Q1"Why not just use walk time plus wait time? Why is a third term needed?"
AWalk + wait captures the average experienced cost. But at peripheral suburban nodes with 60-min headways, a 6-min walk carries ~40% probability of missing the train — incurring another 60 minutes. Ignoring this dramatically underestimates structural burden exactly where inequity is greatest. The Pmiss term converts reliability risk into equivalent time, making TFI sensitive to low-frequency peripheral links.
Q2"Why not use survey-calibrated disutility weights (e.g. 2.5× for waiting)?"
ADisutility weights are context-, mode, and culture-specific — calibrating them requires extensive stated preference surveys beyond the GTFS-only scope of this study. TFI deliberately uses time-consistent, objective components to enable reproducible comparison across 8,000+ edges without subjective weighting. This also preserves transferability to other Indian cities without recalibration.
Q3"The Pmiss formula is a heuristic — is it empirically validated?"
ACorrect — it is a structured heuristic grounded in two literature-validated structural relationships (Liu & Sinha, 2007): miss probability increases with walk duration AND headway length. The clip bounds [0.05, 0.40] prevent unrealistic extremes. Its purpose is to rank and compare interchange nodes by friction burden — not to produce exact probability values. Exact calibration would require real-time arrival data and a stochastic simulation — a clearly stated limitation of this study.
Q4"How is TFI different from existing GTFS-based accessibility metrics?"
AGTFS accessibility tools (r5r, OpenTripPlanner, peartree) answer: "where can you reach in 30 minutes?" — a demand-side reachability question from an origin. TFI answers: "how much hidden time penalty exists at each structural transfer node in the supply network?" — a supply-side interchange performance diagnostic. The two are complementary: accessibility tools show where you can go; TFI reveals where the system structurally fails passengers at the point of transfer.
