Research Problem: Urban transit planning in Chennai optimises spatial connectivity while largely ignoring the temporal burden passengers endure when transferring between modes. Peak-hour schedules remain uncoordinated across CMRL, MTC and Southern Railway, creating invisible time penalties at interchange nodes.
⚠
Research Gap & Objectives
  • 1
    No unified TFI metric exists for Indian cities that integrates walk time, wait time, and missed connection probability into a single scalar.
  • 2
    Spatial inequity unmeasured: Peripheral zones vs. central core transfer burdens have never been quantified using network-level Gini analysis for Chennai.
  • 3
    Gate-to-gate accuracy: Existing studies use centroid distances; precise station entry/exit polygon data is not incorporated.
  • 4
    Policy counterfactuals lacking: No simulation compares Schedule Synchronisation vs. Physical Infrastructure (FOBs) improvements on temporal equity.

Transfer Friction Index (TFI) — Core Metric
The Novel Metric Developed in this Thesis
TFI = Wt + Ww + (Pmiss × Ww)
Wt
Walk Time
gate-to-gate seconds
Ww
Average Wait Time
headway-based proxy
Pmiss
Missed Connection
probability [0.05–0.40]
Probability Formulation

Pmiss = clip[ (Walkt/600) × (Waitw/1800), 0.05, 0.40 ]

High-frequency stops (Metro) receive low Pmiss; peripheral suburban stops with long headways receive high penalties. Default wait = 15 min where headway data is sparse.

Temporal Analysis Windows

🔴 PEAK Hours

08:00–10:00
17:00–19:00

Morning and Evening commute windows. Highest demand, most coordinated but temporally strained. Peripheral nodes show extreme friction spikes.

🔵 OFF-PEAK Hours

10:00–17:00
19:00–08:00

Midday & night windows. Suburban rail off-peak shows severe instability in headways, amplifying TFI beyond peak at many nodes.

🔬
Key Methodological Innovations
A

Gate-to-Gate Accuracy

Entry/exit polygon geometries replace centroid-to-centroid distances — fundamental for accurate physical walk impedance at metro & rail stations.

B

Probabilistic Missed Connection

Unlike static studies, TFI includes a probabilistic term that penalises low-frequency links — capturing real passenger risk of missing connections.

C

Peak / Off-Peak Segmentation

Temporal segmentation of 1.7M records reveals how friction distribution shifts dramatically between peak and off-peak periods — invisible in aggregate analysis.

D

Gini-Based Equity Metric

Adapts welfare economics Gini Coefficient to measure spatial inequality of transfer burden — operationalises equity as a measurable urban planning indicator.
