Detailed Research Design & Conceptual Framework

A systematic blueprint of the research architecture — epistemological position, six research phases, central research questions, theoretical grounding, conceptual flow, variable architecture, and data strategy for measuring Transfer Friction & Temporal Inequity in Chennai's multimodal public transport network.

01Research Phase Architecture — Six Phases
P1
Problem Framing & Conceptualisation

Identify temporal gaps in Chennai transit planning. Situate the TFI as a measurable construct. Premise the research within Indian urban planning literature.

P2
Data Acquisition & Standardisation

GTFS ingestion for CMRL, MTC, and Southern Railway. Filter 1.7M stop-times to Weekday services. Parse calendar.txt. Load gate polygon GeoJSON.

P3
Network Construction

Build time-expanded directed graph. Snap geometries to gate polygons. Compute ~8,000 walk transfer edges using gate-to-gate impedance ≤ 500 m.

P4
TFI Computation & Temporal Segmentation

Calculate per-node headways. Apply TFI formula segmented by Peak (08–10, 17–19) and Off-Peak windows across all intermodal edges.

P5
Equity Analysis & Spatial Aggregation

Compute Gini Coefficient of TFI. Link ward-level demographics. Identify spatial friction hotspots and zone classifications via GIS.

P6
Policy Simulation & Recommendations

Simulate Scenario A (Timetable Sync), B (FOB Infrastructure), C (Combined). Derive planning proposals. Quantify TFI improvement per scenario.

02Central Research Questions
RQ1

Magnitude of Transfer Friction

To what extent do intermodal transfer waiting times, walking distances, and missed connection probabilities generate measurable passenger time penalties at interchange nodes across Chennai's multimodal network?

RQ2

Spatial Distribution of TFI

Does the distribution of TFI across Chennai's interchange nodes reflect spatial inequity? Do peripheral zones systematically bear higher transfer burdens than central core areas?

RQ3

Temporal Variability in TFI

Do peak-hour conditions versus off-peak conditions create significantly different TFI profiles? Are certain nodes disproportionately burdened at specific times of day?

RQ4

Conventional Metrics vs. Temporal Reality

In what ways does proximity-based accessibility overestimate effective transit access when temporal reliability is incorporated? What is the magnitude of this overestimation?

RQ5

Policy Effectiveness Comparison

Between operational interventions (timetable synchronisation) and physical infrastructure (FOBs), which yields greater TFI reduction — and for which population groups?

03Epistemological & Philosophical Position
Post-Positivist Ontology
The reality of temporal inequity in transit is measurable and exists independently of the observer. GTFS schedule data provides objective evidence of structural transfer friction. Measurement precision is the primary task.
Quantitative Epistemology
Knowledge is produced through systematic measurement, deductive reasoning, and statistical comparison. The TFI metric operationalises abstract inequity into a scalar, reproducible value across 8,000+ edges.
Applied Policy Orientation
Research outputs are explicitly designed for applicability in urban transport planning practice — contributing to governance frameworks and data-driven policy design for Indian metropolitan transit systems.
04Theoretical Framework — Four Pillars
Hägerstrand (1970) · Time Geography
Space-Time Trajectories
Travel behaviour as trajectories in space-time. Transfer friction modelled as a temporal constraint — time paths disrupted by schedule misalignment at interchange nodes.
Watts & Strogatz · Network Theory
Directed Graph Analysis
Multimodal transit as a directed graph. Betweenness centrality identifies critical nodes. Time-expanded network captures schedule feasibility as graph topology.
Pereira et al. (2017) · Transport Equity
Accessibility as a Social Good
Gini-based distribution of TFI operationalises welfare economics to assess distributional fairness of transit access across spatial population groups.
GTFS Multimodal Analysis
Schedule-Based Accessibility
GTFS as standardised data layer. Time-expanded networks derived from GTFS enable computational modelling of schedule-dependent accessibility at network scale.
