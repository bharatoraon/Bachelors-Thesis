Transfer Friction & Temporal Inequity in Chennai’s Multimodal Public Transport Network

This research develops and applies the Transfer Friction Index (TFI) — a computation-ready metric integrating walk time, wait time, and missed-connection probability — to evaluate intermodal transfer performance across 642 hubs in Greater Chennai. Schedule misalignment between independently operated agencies (CMRL, MTC, Southern Railway) creates invisible time penalties that disproportionately affect transit-dependent commuters in the city’s periphery. Three policy scenarios are modelled, with combined timetable synchronisation and infrastructure upgrades reducing mean TFI by 23%.
